Generating Audio Illusions with Diffusion Models

Inspired by the use of diffusion models to create optical illusions, we extend this idea to the auditory domain by proposing a method for synthesizing audio illusions which can be played in various directions. We show samples which can be played in the forward or flipped (inverted frequency) directions. Code is available here.

Method

We leverage a pre-trained text-to-spectrogram latent diffusion model to perform zero-shot generation of audio illusions. During the reverse diffusion process, we combine noise estimates derived from transformed noisy latent representations to denoise the spectrogram. This enables the generation of audio samples that align with different textual prompts depending on how they are played.
